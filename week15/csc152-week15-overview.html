Week 15: Disaster Recovery & Backup Solutions Week 15 of 17

Welcome to Week 15! You have spent fourteen weeks building, connecting, managing, and securing a multi-server infrastructure. This week asks the essential question: "How do we protect all of this work against loss?" You will learn disaster recovery principles, implement automated backup solutions, verify that your backups actually work, and explore high availability concepts that keep systems running when failures occur.

Week 15 Learning Goals

By the end of this week you will be able to:

  1. Describe disaster recovery principles including Recovery Time Objective (RTO), Recovery Point Objective (RPO), and Business Impact Analysis (BIA).
  2. Compare and contrast full, incremental, and differential backup types and select appropriate strategies for given scenarios.
  3. Design a backup schedule that balances storage requirements, recovery speed, and data protection needs.
  4. Implement automated backup solutions on Linux and Windows servers using native tools and scripting.
  5. Perform backup verification and restoration procedures to validate backup integrity.
  6. Explain high availability and fault tolerance concepts (clustering, failover, replication) at an awareness level.
  7. Identify considerations for backing up and recovering log and monitoring data (Splunk) as part of a comprehensive DR plan.
  8. Apply disaster recovery and backup concepts to Project 3 deliverables, documenting a backup strategy for the lab infrastructure.

Weeks 1–14 took you through building server infrastructure, securing access and identities, connecting systems to the network, managing storage, implementing directory services, virtualizing environments, and hardening security. Week 15 completes the protection layer by ensuring you can recover from failures—whether caused by hardware faults, human error, or cyberattacks. Looking ahead, Week 16 (System Monitoring & Troubleshooting) uses the protected, monitored infrastructure you have built to practice structured diagnosis and problem resolution. The confidence to troubleshoot aggressively comes from knowing your backups are solid.
