üéØ Discussion Purpose

Welcome to CSC 152! This discussion is your opportunity to introduce yourself to your classmates and instructor. Building a supportive learning community is essential for success in this course. Getting to know each other will make collaboration easier, discussions more engaging, and the semester more enjoyable.

This is a friendly, informal discussion‚Äîthink of it as the first day of class where we go around the room and introduce ourselves. Be authentic, share what you're comfortable sharing, and show interest in your classmates' backgrounds and goals.

üìù Discussion Prompt

Create a post introducing yourself to the class. Your introduction should help us get to know you as a person, understand your background and interests, and learn about your goals for this course.

Include the following in your introduction (Try to answer these questions but you don't have to include anything you don't feel comfortable sharing with us.):

1. Basic Introduction
What's your name, and what should we call you? Where are you from?
2. Academic Background
What's your major or career interest? What year are you in your program?
3. Technology Experience
What's your experience with computers and technology? Have you worked with servers before, or is this completely new territory?
4. Course Goals
What do you hope to learn in this course? What specific skills or knowledge are you most excited about?
5. Personal Touch
Share something interesting about yourself! This could be a hobby, a fun fact, something you're passionate about, or anything else that makes you unique.
6. Ice Breaker Question
If you could have any server superpower (instant backups, perfect troubleshooting intuition, telepathic communication with hardware, etc.), what would it be and why?
üí° Writing Tips
  • Be genuine and conversational‚Äîwrite like you're talking to new friends
  • Share enough detail to be interesting, but only what you're comfortable sharing
  • Use paragraphs to organize your thoughts
  • Proofread before posting‚Äîfirst impressions matter!
  • Aim for 150-250 words (about 2-3 paragraphs)
